NFL Project Presentation

Jason Lin, Matthew Goetting, Patrick Shukis, and Ryan Ji

Background

  • All of us have interests corresponding with professional sports.
  • Of course, we wanted to cater our final project centering around sports statistics.
  • NFLFastR scrapes public NFL play-by-play data into a reliable R package
  • NFLFastR is an ideal foundation for studying how offensive strategy has evolved in the league

History

  • The early years of the league were built around the run game.
  • Over time, the NFL introduced a series of rules that helped open up the field, protect receivers and quarterbacks, and encourage more passing.
  • Kicker Accuracy improved over time along with various rule changes, affecting how kicking is utilized in offensive strategy
  • As passing efficiency skyrocketed, offensive efficiency became more of a numbers game, prioritizing positive value per play

Our Mission

  • Understand the changes in offensive strategy in the time period between 2000 to 2025
  • Discern the effects of certain rule changes on NFL offense
  • Present our findings to NFL Executives, Teams, and Analysts

Approach

  • Use NFLFastR play-by-play data to build a consistent and reproducible dataset
  • Define key offensive metrics for comparison across eras
  • Create visualizations that highlight league-wide shifts in behavior rather than team-specific noise
  • Interpret results in the context of rule changes and broader analytical trends in football

Generating Our SQLite

#| echo: true
years <- 2000:2025  
dcon <- dbConnect(RSQLite::SQLite(), "nfl_pbp.sqlite")

# creates tables for specific years (2000-2025)
for(year in years){
  message("Loading ", year, "...")
  pbp <- nflfastR::load_pbp(year)
  table_name <- paste0("pbp_", year)
  
  dbWriteTable(dcon, table_name, pbp, overwrite = TRUE)
  
  message("Created Table: ", table_name)
}

# creates the general play_by_play data from all years
all_queries <- paste0("SELECT * FROM pbp_", years)
union_query <- paste(all_queries, collapse = " UNION ALL ")

dbExecute(dcon, paste0("CREATE TABLE play_by_play AS ", union_query))
